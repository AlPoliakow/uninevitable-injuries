Shoulder injury

Case: 29 y.o highly active individual had a fall from a bike whilst crossing tram tracks in the rain. Landed on left shoulder and neck and slid several metres pushing the shoulder into internal rotation and depression.
Presenting with general shoulder pain, inability to flex shoulder past 40 degrees and inability to externally rotate beyond -20 degrees.
No numbness/tingling/burning.

Tests:

  • Range of movement
  • Palpation
  • X-ray
  • Ultrasound
  • Strength
  • Specific tests

Reasoning:

  • Potential teres minor tear (incomplete) would explain pain/difficulty with external rotation and loss of shoulder flexion/abduction range
  • This hypothesis is supported by pain on internal rotation (stretch) and pain on contraction
  • Generalised pain on palpation not a specific indicator
  • Long head of biceps cleared
  • Neck cleared to rule out referred pain contribution
  • Subscapularis injury possible from testing but unlikely from mechanism of injury

Working diagnosis:
Subacromial bursa inflammation,
Posterior labrum inflammation,
and grade 1-2 teres minor tear

Treatment plan:

  • Rest, using pain as a limiting factor
  • Short term sling use
  • Short term ibuprofen and paracetamol
  • Pain free active assisted range of movement (until full pain free ROM)
  • Pain free active ROM (until full pain free ROM) then add weight/resistance
  • Isometric, pain free shoulder external rotation, shoulder internal rotation, elbow flexion and elbow extension
  • Neutral shoulder, straight arm dumbbell hold
  • Shoulder external rotation sustained into shoulder flexion range
  • Inrease resistance and load as pain allows
  • Progress to functional exercises integrating the kinetic chain
  • Unilateral strengthening of unaffected upper limb with maximal load as pain allows
  • Maintain cardiovascular fitness via non-painful modalities (stationary bike)
  • Maintain lower limb strength with low-risk exercises such as seated knee extension/flexion
  • Maintain core and forearm strength with pain free exercises

Recovery checklist:

  • Normal clinical examination ✓
  • Normal shoulder arthokinetics ✓
  • Normal kinetic chain integration ✓
  • Completed exercise progressions ✓

Recovery status: full recovery, returned to sport

Evidence/references:

  • Shoulder exams - Stanford Medicine
  • Brukner P, & Khan K(Eds.), [2015] Brukner & Khan’s Clinical Sports Medicine: The Medicine of Exercise, 4th Ed, McGraw-Hill Education (Australia) Pty Ltd. PMCID: PMC3941124.
  • Shoulder Special Tests - Physiopedia
  • Physical Examination test of the shoulder (Gismervik et al., 2017)

Knee injury

Case: 29 y. o. highly active individual reports knee pain during sustained and loaded flexion with particular pain issues during prolonged sitting and returning to movement post prolonged sitting. Long-term symptoms with increase in severity after rapid increase in cycling duration from 20 minutes to 2 hours regularly, multiple times weekly.

Tests:

  • Range of movement
  • Palpation/observation
  • Strength
  • Specific tests
  • Functional tests
  • Anterior knee pain scale

Reasoning:

  • Sufficient knee extension strength, disproportionate weakness in hip and knee flexors and mild weakness in hip extensors
  • Eliminated diagnoses: Plica syndrome, Capital Femoral Epiphysis, muscle tears, articular cartilage lesion, Osteochondritis Dissecans of the knee, Patellofemoral instability, Patellar Tendinopathy, Knee Osteoarthritis, Prepatellar Bursitis, Iliotibial band syndrome
  • Fractures unlikely due to symptom severity and pattern

Working diagnosis: Patello-femoral pain

Treatment plan:

  • Exercise therapy with combined hip and knee exercises 1
  • Exercise therapy + foot orthoses or patellar taping or manual therapy
  • Hip flexor strengthenging: straight leg raise resisted hip flexion
  • Hip extensor and abductor strengthenging: bulgarian split squats, single leg bridges, end of range pistol squats
  • Controlled mobility exercises: cossack squats, L-sit leg raises (hamstrings), Prasarita Padottanasana
  • Avoid: dry needling, manual therapy, mobilisations in isolation, and electrophysical agents
  • No manual therapy

Recovery checklist:

  • Sitting 30 minutes pain free ×
  • Pain free single leg shrimp squat x5 reps ✓
  • Pain free next day after 2.5km run ✓
  • Pain free next day after 45 min cycle ✓

Recovery status: recovery in progress

Evidence/references:

  • Exercise and Physiotherapy interventions for Patello-femoral knee pain
  • Patello-femoral Pain (Clinical Practice Guidelines 2019)
  • Plica syndrome
  • Q-Angle
  • Chondromalacia Patellae
  • Sinding Larsen Johansson Syndrome
  • Philidelphia Clinical Practice Guidelines on selected interventions for knee pain
  • Femoral Nerve Tension Test
  • Iliotibial Band Syndrome
