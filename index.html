Shoulder injury

Case: 29 y.o highly active individual had a fall from a bike whilst crossing tram tracks in the rain. Landed on left shoulder and neck and slid several metres pushing the shoulder into internal rotation and depression.
Presenting with general shoulder pain, inability to flex shoulder past 40 degrees and inability to externally rotate beyond -20 degrees.

Tests:

  • Range of movement
  • Palpation
  • X-ray
  • Ultrasound
  • Strength
  • Specific tests

Reasoning:

  • Potential teres minor tear (incomplete) would explain pain/difficulty with external rotation and loss of shoulder flexion/abduction range
  • This hypothesis is supported by pain on internal rotation (stretch) and pain on contraction
  • Generalised pain on palpation not a specific indicator

Working diagnosis: subacromial bursa inflammation, posterior labrum inflammation, and grade 1-2 teres minor tear

Treatment plan:

  • Rest, using pain as a limiting factor
  • Short term sling use and short term ibuprofen and paracetamol
  • Gradual return to pain free ROM
  • Isometric, pain free shoulder external rotation strengthening after 2 weeks rest
  • Shoulder external rotation sustained into shoulder flexion range
  • Increased resistance and load as pain allows
  • Pain free strengthening progression into ROM

Recovery checklist:

  • Normal clinical examination
  • Normal shoulder arthokinetics
  • Normal kinetic chain integration
  • Completed exercise progressions

Knee injury

Case:Active 29 y o reports knee pain during sustained and loaded flexion with particular pain during prolonged sitting and returning to movement. Only noted preceding factor is a rapid increase in cycling duration for 20 minutes to 2 hours regularly

Tests:

  • Range of movement
  • Palpation/observation
  • Strength
  • Specific tests
  • Functional tests
  • Anterior knee pain scale

Reasoning: sufficient knee extension strength, disproportionate weakness in hip and knee flexors and mild weakness in hip extensors

Working diagnosis: Patello-femoral pain

Treatment plan:

  • Exercise therapy with combined hip and knee exercises 1
  • Exercise therapy + foot orthoses or patellar taping or manual therapy
  • Hip flexor strengthenging: straight leg raise resisted hip flexion
  • Hip extensor and abductor strengthenging: bulgarian split squats, single leg bridges, end of range pistol squats
  • Controlled mobility exercises: cossack squats, L-sit leg raises (hamstrings), Prasarita Padottanasana
  • No dry needling
  • No manual therapy

Tips: electrophysical agents not recommended, mobilisations not recommended in isolation (1)

Recovery checklist:

  • Sitting 30 minutes pain free
  • Pain free full range single leg squat
